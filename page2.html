More Info
  • The Treaty of Waitangi was created to secure British sovereignty over New Zealand and was then copied and transluted to Māori by missionary Henry Williams and his son Edward.
  • Around 500 Maori chiefs debated the signing of the treaty for a day and a night before it was first signed on february 6 1840.
  • Hōno Heke and about fourty other chiefs were the first Māori to sign the treaty on feburary 6, and by september of the same year another 500 had signed the copies that were sent throughout the country.
  • Britain proclaimed sovereignty over the country on 21 May 1840.
  • The Treaty is a statement of principles on which the British and Māori made a pact to found a nation state and build a government in New Zealand.
